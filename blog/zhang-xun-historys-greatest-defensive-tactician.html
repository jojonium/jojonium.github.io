Zhang Xun: History's Greatest Defensive Tactician

Zhang Xun was a Chinese general during the Tang Dynasty, and one of my favorite historical figures. He used brilliant tactics to successfully defend two fortresses against overwhelming enemy strength, displaying extreme loyalty to his dynasty and protecting the southern China from being conquered by Yan rebels practically single-handedly. Although he resorted to some unfortunate strategies, such as condoning mass cannibalism, Zhang is still a fascinating figure with a life story that reads like a movie script.

Background

The Tang Dynasty was a powerful empire that ruled China from 618 to 907, often regarded as a golden age in Chinese history. The first half of the dynasty's reign was a period of stability and relative peace. Unfortunately, this stability ended in 755 when a Tang general named An Lushan declared himself emperor of northern China, establishing a rival dynasty known as Yan.

At first the Yan made quick gains in conquering China. An Lushan treated captured Tang generals and officials well, leading more and more of them to rebel and join his side.
